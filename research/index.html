Buoyancy-Driven Entrainment

The mechanism of entrainment has long been thought to originate from turbulent eddies. There are some flows however, where entrainment persists even in the laminar regime. “Thermals”, or regions of isolated buoyant fluid thought to be the basic unit of convection (Yano, 2014), were recently found to vary in entraining rates by less than 20% in laminar (Re=630) and turbulent (Re=6300) simulations in a dry atmosphere (Lecoanet and Jeevanjee, 2018). We derive a simple formula that reproduces these results. We then demonstrate that by removing gravity midway through a simulation, the entrainment rate drops dramatically, giving direct evidence of the central role of buoyancy in entrainment. See McKim et al. (2019).
